NMBR1

Animation Chararacter Design

Overview

Made as a project for Simon Fraser University’s Animation class, NMBR1 is a narrative driven animated music video created with four other students over the course of roughly 10 weeks.

Roles: 3D Modelling | Character Designer

Timeline: Spring 2025

Tools: Blender | Figma | Photoshop

Goal:
Create a high-energy, vibrant, and fun music video demonstrating the principles of animation and showcasing our knowledge of course materials.

Concepts

After selecting a song, we decided we wanted to create a highly-stylized, neon street race. We wanted to make our characters very distinct in personality and design in order to make each character memorable and convey as much information since we couldn’t provide any additional context or dialogue. With this in mind, we each sketched out some characters aligned with our goal, as well as some potential shots that matched the lyrics of the song and built up the path for the narrative.

Modeling

After some team critique and revisions, character designs were finalized and we began modeling our characters in Blender. Because I had some prior experience modelling and rigging in Blender, I was able to make good progress on my character, Leon, while also occasionally helping my teammates with their models.

Animating

Once Leon’s model was mostly complete, I made some short animations testing the model to get me comfortable with the process (fig. 6), as I had little experience actually animating in Blender. We planned out a shot list with their lengths, lyrics, and other information, and divided them amongst ourselves. A big focus for each of us was making our animation feel energetic and timing whatever we could to the beat of our selected song.

Challenges

There were few real challenges we encountered working on this project as my team and I worked very well together and we were able to help each other if we struggled. That being said, we did encounter some issues getting Blender’s physics engine to work the way we intended it to in the couple of shots where we were using it. In some cases we were just able to render the shot on a more powerful computer, but in others we had to rework the shot to work better.

Outcome

My team and I received a high grade for our project and was regarded with praise from our professor and TA. NMBR 1 was even included as an example of a good project in at least the two semesters following ours where it was voted most enjoyable by the students in these semesters. Overall, this project was a very positive team experience for me and I took away from it strategies to structure and distribute tasks for projects made in groups as well as animation skills that I have been applying to my personal game development projects.
