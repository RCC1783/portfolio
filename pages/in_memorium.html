In Memoriam

Games | Games | <Game Design>

Overview

In Memoriam is a story driven 3D action platformer built in Unity for Simon Fraser University’s Foundations of Game Design class. The aim of the project was to create a game with a branching narrative structure while applying concepts such as player types as well as core and satellite mechanics.

Art Dev

Prior to production, I contributed to the art of In Memoriam with a concept design for the player character (as shown in figure 5), and some rough storyboards for narrative beats (as shown in figures 6 and 7). Although my character design did not make it through to the end, elements of the design such as clothing genre were kept.

Process

My focus initially was to implement the foundational mechanics of our game as an action platformer: movement and camera controls. From here, I moved onto combat, health, and enemy behaviours (as shown in figure 8) that could be inherited from to create a variety of enemies. 

Although the game’s scope was relatively small, I wanted to create an easily expandable system to make every enemy different. Figure 9 shows the result, a completed enemy and its editable properties. Were I to work on this project further, I would easily be able to add more unique behaviours to each enemy such as more complicated or unique attacks.

Outside of programming, I was also the most experienced in using Unity and did much of the work bringing over the art and animation created by our group's lead artist. Figure 8 shows some of this process as I use the sprites created by the team’s artist.

Challenges

Because the player is 2D in a 3D environment and we only had sprites from one camera perspective, playtesters struggled to gauge where the player was going to land after a jump. To fix this, I moved the player character’s light closer to their feet so players could use it as a shadow. This change can be seen when comparing Figures 11 and 12. This somewhat decreased the issue however, If I continued with the project, I would include sprites from different camera angles as well.

Outcome

Developing In Memoriam put my programming and Unity skills to the test. Designing and programming the game’s mechanics was a big undertaking and using playtesting to improve the project helped me take away a lot from this project.
